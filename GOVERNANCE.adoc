= Governance Model
:toc: preamble

This document describes how `epistemic-types` (`hyperpolymath/epistemic-types`)
is governed. It is a small, deliberately honest Agda formalization, so the
model is intentionally lightweight and matches the size of the project.

== Overview

`epistemic-types` follows a **single-maintainer, benevolent-steward** model.

* One maintainer, **Jonathan D.A. Jewell (hyperpolymath)**
  `<j.d.a.jewell@open.ac.uk>`, holds final authority over the project.
* The maintainer acts as a steward: the goal is to keep the formalization
  small, honest, and type-checking, not to grow a large API.
* Proposals, issues, and pull requests are welcome and are reviewed on
  technical merit.
* Decisions are recorded in the open, through GitHub issues, pull requests,
  and `CHANGELOG.md`.

The project's stewardship bias is the same one stated in `readme.adoc`: it
**favours mathematical honesty over a large API**. Governance exists mainly to
protect that bias.

== Core principles

[cols="1,2"]
|===
| Principle | Description

| **Honesty over surface area**
| A change that adds convenience but hides an unproved assumption is declined.
  Postulates and a dependence on the Agda standard library are out of scope by
  design (the build runs `agda --no-libraries` under `--safe --without-K`).

| **Stewardship, not gatekeeping**
| The maintainer has the final say but prefers to merge well-scoped, honest
  contributions and to explain declines.

| **Merit**
| Contributions are judged on the mathematics and the proof, not on who sent
  them.

| **Transparency**
| Significant decisions are documented in the issue, PR, or `CHANGELOG.md`.

| **Open contribution**
| Anyone may fork and open a pull request.
|===

== Roles

|===

| **Maintainer / steward**

There are no co-maintainers at present. The project is a prototype
(version 0.1.0), and a single steward is the right size for it.

== Graduated trust (kept lightweight)

Rather than a formal committee structure, trust is graduated by track record,
and kept deliberately informal:

. **First contribution** — reviewed closely by the maintainer; the bar is that
  it type-checks under `just check` and does not introduce a postulate or a
  hidden assumption.
. **Repeat contributor** — a contributor with a history of honest, accurate
  patches gets faster review and the benefit of the doubt on scope.
. **Trusted reviewer (future)** — if contribution volume ever warrants it, the
  maintainer may invite a trusted contributor to review or co-maintain. This
  has not happened yet and is not promised; it is recorded here only so the
  path is visible.

This mirrors, very loosely, the project's own subject matter: the
`ProofTransport` module is precisely about not smuggling proofhood across a
trust boundary. Governance does the same thing socially — trust is earned and
checked locally, never assumed.

== Decision making

=== Routine decisions

* Documentation fixes (in `readme.adoc`, `explainme.adoc`, or the other docs)
* Small proof clarifications that keep the same statements
* Typos, comment fixes, formatting

**Process:** the maintainer reviews and merges if it type-checks and is honest.

=== Significant changes

* New modules under `src/EpistemicTypes/`
* New interfaces (for example, a new modality record alongside `Base`)
* Changes to a public statement, signature, or law
* New bridge modules toward `echo-types`, `ephapax`, or the `a2ml`/`k9`
  engineering rendering in `.machine_readable/proof-transport/`

**Process:**

. Open an issue describing the change and the mathematics it commits to.
. Discuss; the maintainer checks it against the "honesty over API" bias and
  against what `readme.adoc` lists as *intentionally unformalized*.
. The maintainer decides and records the rationale in the issue or PR.

=== Structural decisions

* Repository purpose, renaming, or archival
* Licence classification
* Ownership transfer

**Process:** the maintainer decides, and the decision is recorded in
`CHANGELOG.md`. Licence and SPDX matters are handled manually by the owner and
are out of scope for automated changes.

== Contribution lifecycle

[cols="1,2"]
|===
| Stage | What happens

| **Idea**
| Open an issue. Worth checking the *roadmap* and *what remains intentionally
  unformalized* sections of `readme.adoc` first — some omissions are deliberate.

| **Development**
| Fork, implement, and make it type-check: `just check`
  (i.e. `agda --no-libraries -i src src/EpistemicTypes/All.agda`, Agda 2.8.0).
  No postulates; no Agda standard library.

| **Review**
| Open a PR. The maintainer reviews on a best-effort basis (this is a personal
  research prototype, not a staffed project).

| **Merge**
| The maintainer merges or requests changes.

| **Release**
| The maintainer tags and updates `CHANGELOG.md`. There is no automated release
  pipeline.
|===

== Conflict resolution

. Discuss in the relevant issue or PR, with the mathematics on the table.
. The maintainer mediates and decides.
. The decision is documented and may be revisited later if new evidence or a
  cleaner proof appears.

== Repository conventions

[cols="1,2"]
|===
| Convention | Description

| **Build**
| `just check` is the single source of truth. A change that does not
  type-check is not merged.

| **No postulates, no stdlib**
| The formalization stays constructive and self-contained
  (`Agda.Builtin.*` / `Agda.Primitive` only).

| **Docs**
| AsciiDoc (`.adoc`) is the default. Existing docs keep their lowercase names
  (`readme.adoc`, `explainme.adoc`). Only `CONTRIBUTING.md`,
  `CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`, `SECURITY.md`, and `CHANGELOG.md` are Markdown, for
  GitHub community-health recognition.

| **Machine-readable**
| `a2ml` + Nickel/`k9` artefacts live under `.machine_readable/`, including the
  engineering rendering of `ProofTransport` in
  `.machine_readable/proof-transport/`.

| **CI/CD**
| Not enabled. There are no GitHub Actions workflows or status badges; see
  `AUDIT.adoc` for the reasons.
|===

== Ecosystem position

`epistemic-types` is a sibling, not a dependency, of the related estate
formalizations. The `EchoBridge` module is written so it can compose with
`echo-types` (the loss-with-residue formalism) without importing it; `ephapax`
shares the echo-obligation framing; the `a2ml`/`k9` tooling in `standards` is
the engineering target for `ProofTransport`. Governance of those repositories
is separate and lives in their own `GOVERNANCE` documents.

== Governance evolution

If the project grows, this model may change — for example by adding a
co-maintainer or a trusted reviewer (see *graduated trust* above). Any such
change is itself a structural decision and is recorded in `CHANGELOG.md`.
